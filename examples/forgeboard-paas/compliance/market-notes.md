# Market Notes

This file is a product design aid, not legal advice.

## EU

EU-first buyers need strong defaults for operational resilience, accessibility, data minimisation, audit evidence, and AI-assisted decision logging. Fintech buyers are especially sensitive to DORA-shaped evidence and third-party ICT risk.

## US

US buyers usually ask for NIST-aligned AI/security governance, SOC 2 evidence, ADA/WCAG accessibility posture, and sector requirements such as HIPAA or PCI DSS. Public companies may need cybersecurity incident evidence suitable for disclosure workflows.

## UK

UK buyers often map to UK GDPR, FCA/PRA operational resilience for financial services, NHS DSP Toolkit expectations for healthcare, and WCAG accessibility posture for public-sector or enterprise procurement.

## Global Enterprise

Multinational buyers usually need a procurement-friendly baseline: ISO 27001 mapping, SOC 2 evidence, WCAG AA posture, data residency controls, customer-managed keys, and air-gap support.
